Part 5 — Exam-style synthesis question
12 marks
Q14 EXAM STYLE
12 marks
Scenario A large international school in Seoul is installing an automated greenhouse on the roof to grow vegetables for the cafeteria. The greenhouse must:

• Maintain air temperature between 18–24°C using heaters and motorised roof vents
• Water the plants automatically when soil moisture drops below a set threshold
• Open or close the blinds depending on how much sunlight is hitting the leaves
• Display the current temperature, moisture and light readings on a screen for the gardener

The gardener wants the system to be reliable, energy-efficient, and able to adapt to changing weather conditions throughout the year.
Discuss how this automated greenhouse can be designed as a control system. Justify your choices throughout.
Mark scheme — your answer should include • Identification of input, process, output and feedback for at least one of the three sub-systems
• Specific sensors you would use, and what each one measures
• Specific actuators the controller would send commands to
• An example of a transducer in the system and why it is needed
• A clear explanation of why closed-loop feedback is essential here
• A brief comment on the control algorithm — simple on/off vs PID, and why
